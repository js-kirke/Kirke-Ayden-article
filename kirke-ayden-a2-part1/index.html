Last Updated: July 2019

July 1947

On July 8th, 1947, an unidentified flying object crash landed just outside the town of Roswell, New Mexico in a town called Corona. The incident is known as the Roswell incident because it was the closest town with a police station, and the near by military base with the same name only added to the mysterious events that occurred.

The farmer who discovered the wreckage, as well as several onlookers described the material as "liquid metal", and there are reports that the witnesses touched and played with the material. Once the farmer brought the wreckage home, he woke up his family in order to show them, however, they also had an uninvited guest. The farmer reported that he was visited by two pale men in black suits who somehow convinced him to let them in, and before he knew it, the strangers left, taking the wreckage and replacing it with metal rods and foil, all the materials of a weather balloon

Initially written off as a weather balloon, the story faded into obscurity for thirty years, until 1978. Stanton Friedman, a researcher of aliens and UFO's, began conducting interviews and collecting data, including:

  • First hand reports
  • Documents received through the "Freedom of Information Act"
  • Other leaked documents such as programs like Majestic 12

Aftermath

This incident was the first glimpse into the extraterrestrial world for the American public, and is very well known world-wide. The town of Roswell now uses tourist attractions that aim to attract UFO fanatics worldwide.
